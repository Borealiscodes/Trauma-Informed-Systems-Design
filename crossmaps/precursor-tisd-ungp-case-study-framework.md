# **Precursor Framework for Formal Crossmap  
Trauma‑Informed‑Systems‑Design (TISD) × UN Guiding Principles on Business & Human Rights (UNGPs) × Case Studies**

## **1. Purpose of the Crossmap**
This precursor establishes the conceptual structure for a formal crossmap between:

- Trauma‑Informed‑Systems‑Design (TISD)  
- The UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs)  
- Documented case studies involving automated enforcement, account shutdowns, and service failures affecting a neurodivergent human‑rights defender under transnational‑risk conditions.

The full crossmap will analyze how TISD operationalizes, extends, or reveals gaps in UNGP implementation, and how the case studies demonstrate real‑world failure modes relevant to both frameworks.

---

## **2. Core Alignment Axes**
The formal crossmap will be organized around three primary axes:

### **Axis A — Normative Principles**
UNGPs define normative obligations:
- State duty to protect  
- Corporate responsibility to respect  
- Access to remedy  

TISD defines architectural obligations:
- Trauma‑informed invariants  
- Cognitive‑access modeling  
- Holonomy‑based distortion analysis  
- Generator‑based procedural safety  

Case studies provide empirical evidence of:
- State‑linked harm  
- Corporate automation failures  
- Absence of effective remedy  

### **Axis B — Operational Mechanisms**
UNGPs emphasize:
- Human‑rights due diligence  
- Impact assessment  
- Policy commitments  
- Grievance mechanisms  

TISD emphasizes:
- Trauma‑signal detection  
- Predictable communication structures  
- Oversight lanes  
- Evidence‑preservation protocols  

Case studies illustrate:
- Automated misclassification  
- Inaccessible support channels  
- Data loss during enforcement  
- Escalation barriers  

### **Axis C — Vulnerability & Risk**
UNGPs acknowledge:
- Heightened risk for human‑rights defenders  
- Need for special protections  

TISD models:
- Neurodivergent cognition  
- Trauma‑affected decision pathways  
- Holonomy distortions under repression  

Case studies demonstrate:
- Transnational‑risk amplification  
- Cognitive‑load crises  
- Systemic retraumatization  

---

## **3. Structural Components of the Future Crossmap**
The full crossmap will contain:

### **3.1 Principle‑to‑Invariant Mapping**
Each UNGP principle will be mapped to:
- relevant TISD invariants,  
- architectural requirements,  
- and case‑study evidence.

### **3.2 Failure Mode Analysis**
Case studies will be analyzed as:
- violations of UNGP expectations,  
- violations of TISD invariants,  
- and examples of holonomy distortion.

### **3.3 Architectural Remediation Pathways**
For each failure mode, the crossmap will propose:
- trauma‑informed remediation,  
- human‑rights‑aligned system design,  
- enterprise‑level support structures.

### **3.4 NDH‑Based Extensions**
The crossmap will identify where:
- NDH geometry  
- non‑dual algebra  
- and projection‑based modeling  

provide deeper insight into:
- bias,  
- distortion,  
- and systemic harm.

---

## **4. Key Themes to be Developed in the Full Document**

### **4.1 Automation Without Context**
Case studies show how automated systems can:
- misclassify human‑rights work as spam,  
- trigger account shutdowns,  
- destroy evidence,  
- and escalate risk.

The crossmap will analyze how UNGPs require contextual due diligence, and how TISD provides the architectural means to implement it.

### **4.2 Trauma‑Informed Accessibility**
The crossmap will examine:
- cognitive‑load barriers,  
- PTSD‑related constraints,  
- and the need for predictable, written‑only support.

UNGPs require accessibility; TISD defines how to build it.

### **4.3 Transnational‑Risk Amplification**
Case studies demonstrate:
- enforced disappearance,  
- asylum coercion,  
- FOIA obstruction,  
- and cross‑jurisdictional harm.

The crossmap will show how UNGPs anticipate these risks, and how TISD operationalizes protections.

### **4.4 Evidence‑Preservation Obligations**
The crossmap will highlight:
- the need for safe archival,  
- non‑destructive enforcement,  
- and HRD‑flagged accounts.

UNGPs require remedy; TISD defines the architecture for preserving evidence so remedy is possible.

---

## **5. Intended Output of the Future Crossmap**
The final document will produce:

- A full matrix mapping UNGP principles to TISD invariants.  
- A structured analysis of case‑study failures.  
- A trauma‑informed, NDH‑aligned remediation framework.  
- Recommendations for enterprise‑level support systems.  
- A governance‑grade architecture for human‑rights‑aligned AI and cloud services.

This precursor establishes the conceptual scaffolding for that work.
