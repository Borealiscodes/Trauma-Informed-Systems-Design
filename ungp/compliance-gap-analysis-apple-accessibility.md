# **UNGP Compliance Gap Analysis  
Apple Accessibility × Human‑Rights Defender Context × NDH/TISD Architecture**

## **1. Purpose of the Gap Analysis**
This analysis identifies **where Apple’s actual behavior** diverges from:

- its **public human‑rights commitments**,  
- its **accessibility mission**,  
- and its obligations under the **UN Guiding Principles on Business & Human Rights (UNGPs)**.

It builds directly on the **Formal Crossmap** and prepares the evidence base for the **governance escalation letter**.

---

# **2. UNGP Framework Reference Points**

The UNGPs impose three pillars:

1. **State Duty to Protect**  
2. **Corporate Responsibility to Respect**  
3. **Access to Remedy**

Within these, corporations have explicit obligations toward:

- **vulnerable groups**,  
- **neurodivergent individuals**,  
- **trauma‑affected individuals**,  
- **human‑rights defenders (HRDs)**.

This gap analysis evaluates Apple’s behavior against these obligations.

---

# **3. Gap Category I — Failure of Corporate Responsibility to Respect (UNGP Pillar II)**

### **UNGP Requirement**
Corporations must:

- identify human‑rights risks,  
- prevent harm,  
- avoid actions that silence or isolate HRDs,  
- ensure dignity and respect in communication.

### **Apple’s Public Claim**
Apple states it:

- treats everyone with dignity,  
- respects human rights,  
- follows UNGPs,  
- maintains channels with human‑rights experts.

### **Actual Behavior**
Apple responded to a trauma‑informed accessibility submission with:

- an abrupt IP‑protection disclaimer,  
- no recognition of HRD context,  
- no trauma‑informed framing,  
- no alternative pathway.

### **Gap**
Apple failed to meet **UNGP Pillar II** obligations by:

- misclassifying a human‑rights accessibility submission as a product idea,  
- ignoring vulnerability,  
- increasing cognitive load,  
- shutting down communication.

**Severity:** **Critical**  
**Relevant NDH/TISD Invariant:** **trauma‑signal detection**

# **4. Gap Category II — Failure to Provide Access to Remedy (UNGP Pillar III)**

### **UNGP Requirement**
Corporations must:

- provide accessible, non‑judicial grievance mechanisms,  
- ensure non‑discriminatory access to remedy,  
- offer escalation pathways,  
- avoid retraumatizing communication.

### **Apple’s Public Claim**
Apple states it:

- remedies adverse impacts,  
- provides channels for human‑rights concerns,  
- engages with civil‑rights and UN bodies.

### **Actual Behavior**
Apple:

- provided no remedy pathway,  
- offered no escalation route,  
- did not acknowledge harm,  
- did not provide alternative communication modes.

### **Gap**
Apple failed to meet **UNGP Pillar III** obligations by:

- denying access to remedy,  
- failing to provide a safe channel,  
- failing to recognize HRD status,  
- failing to mitigate harm.

**Severity:** **Critical**  
**Relevant NDH/TISD Invariant:** **stability envelope**
